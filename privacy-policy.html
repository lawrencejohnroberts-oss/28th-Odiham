Privacy Policy

28th Odiham (Dogmersfield and Crondall) Scout Group

Registered Charity No. 302221

Last Updated: January 2026

1. Introduction

28th Odiham (Dogmersfield and Crondall) Scout Group ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting and respecting your privacy. This policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

We are a registered charity (No. 302221) and part of The Scout Association. Our registered address is Scout Centre, Pankridge Street, Crondall, Surrey, GU10 5RQ.

2. Data Controller

For the purposes of data protection legislation, 28th Odiham (Dogmersfield and Crondall) Scout Group is the data controller. Our Group Chair is Matthew Seary, who can be contacted at matthew@derwenthillside.co.uk for data protection matters.

3. What Information We Collect

We may collect and process the following types of personal information:

3.1 Young People and Members

  • Name, date of birth, and contact details
  • Parent/guardian contact information
  • Medical information and emergency contacts
  • Dietary requirements and allergies
  • Attendance records and badge progress
  • Photographs and videos (with consent)
  • Behavioural records where necessary

3.2 Adult Volunteers

  • Name, address, and contact details
  • Date of birth and emergency contacts
  • DBS check information
  • Training records and qualifications
  • References and employment history
  • Bank details for expense reimbursements

3.3 Website Visitors

  • Information submitted through contact forms
  • Cookies and website usage data
  • IP addresses and browser information

4. How We Collect Information

We collect information through:

  • Membership application forms
  • Event booking forms
  • Website contact forms
  • Direct communication (email, phone, in person)
  • The Scout Association's membership system (OSM)
  • Photography and filming at events (with consent)

5. Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal data under the following legal bases:

Purpose Legal Basis
Membership administration Legitimate interests / Contract
Safeguarding Legal obligation / Vital interests
Medical information Vital interests / Consent
Photography/filming Consent
Marketing communications Consent
DBS checks Legal obligation

6. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

6.1 Essential Activities

  • Managing membership and attendance
  • Organizing activities and events
  • Ensuring health and safety
  • Safeguarding young people and adults at risk
  • Emergency contact and medical care
  • Badge and award administration

6.2 Communication

  • Sending information about activities and events
  • Emergency communications
  • Newsletters and updates (with consent)
  • Responding to enquiries

6.3 Legal and Regulatory

  • Compliance with Scout Association requirements
  • Meeting charity and legal obligations
  • Insurance and risk management
  • Financial administration

7. Who We Share Information With

We may share personal information with:

7.1 The Scout Association

As required for membership administration, training records, and safeguarding purposes.

7.2 Other Scout Groups and Districts

When young people participate in joint activities or transfer between groups.

7.3 Emergency Services

In medical emergencies or safeguarding situations.

7.4 Service Providers

  • Activity centres and venues
  • Transport providers
  • Insurance companies
  • IT service providers
  • Photography services (with consent)

7.5 Legal Authorities

When required by law, including:

  • Police and social services (safeguarding)
  • Courts and legal proceedings
  • Regulatory bodies
  • DBS and disclosure services

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy:

Type of Data Retention Period
Membership records (active members) Duration of membership + 3 years
Safeguarding records Until the person reaches 25 years + 10 years
Financial records 7 years from end of financial year
DBS certificates 6 months after verification
Training records Duration of appointment + 3 years
Website enquiries 2 years or until resolved

9. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal information:

9.1 Physical Security

  • Locked filing cabinets for paper records
  • Restricted access to storage areas
  • Secure disposal of confidential waste

9.2 Digital Security

  • Password-protected computers and systems
  • Encrypted data transmission
  • Regular software updates and security patches
  • Secure cloud storage with reputable providers
  • Regular data backups

9.3 Access Controls

  • Role-based access to personal information
  • Regular review of user permissions
  • Training for volunteers on data protection

10. International Transfers

We may transfer personal information outside the UK/EEA in the following circumstances:

  • International Scout events and exchanges
  • Cloud storage services with appropriate safeguards
  • Emergency situations abroad

Any transfers will be made with appropriate safeguards in place, including adequacy decisions or standard contractual clauses.

Under GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right of Access: Request a copy of your personal information
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your information (subject to legal requirements)
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your information
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your information in a portable format
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for processing (where applicable)

11. Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of your rights, please contact us using the details below. We will respond within one month of receiving your request.

Please note that some rights may be limited where we have a legal obligation to retain information (e.g., safeguarding records) or where information is necessary for the performance of our charitable activities.

12. Cookies and Website Data

Our website uses cookies to improve your browsing experience:

12.1 Essential Cookies

These are necessary for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled.

12.2 Analytics Cookies

We may use analytics tools to understand how visitors use our website. This helps us improve our services.

12.3 Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. However, disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

13. Photography and Social Media

We may take photographs and videos at Scout activities for:

  • Celebrating achievements and memories
  • Promoting Scouting activities
  • Website and social media content
  • Newsletters and publications

We will always seek appropriate consent before taking or using photographs, particularly of young people. Parents/guardians can withdraw consent at any time.

14. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. Please review their privacy policies before providing any personal information.

15. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of any significant changes and publish the updated policy on our website.
