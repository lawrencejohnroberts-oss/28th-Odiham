Safeguarding Policy

28th Odiham (Dogmersfield and Crondall) Scout Group

Registered Charity No. 302221

Last Updated: January 2026

1. Policy Statement

It is the policy of The Scout Association and 28th Odiham (Dogmersfield and Crondall) Scout Group to safeguard the welfare of all children, young people and adults at risk by protecting them from neglect and from physical, sexual and emotional harm.

We understand that individuals thrive in safe surroundings, so we are committed to ensuring that Scouting is safe and enjoyable for everyone involved and that safeguarding practice reflects statutory responsibilities, government guidance and complies with best practice and Charity Commission requirements.

Our policy aims to ensure that all children, young people and adults at risk have a positive and enjoyable Scouting experience in a safe and person-centred environment and are protected from abuse whilst participating in Scouts and otherwise.

2. Our Commitment

As adults in Scouts, the safety of young people is our priority. We're committed to:

  • Taking the interests and well-being of young people into account, in all our considerations and activities
  • Respecting the rights, wishes and feelings of the young people with whom we work
  • Taking all reasonable practicable steps to protect them from neglect, physical, sexual and emotional abuse
  • Promoting the welfare of young people and their protection within a position of trust

3. Responsibilities

3.1 All Adults

It is the responsibility of all adults involved in 28th Odiham (Dogmersfield and Crondall) Scout Group to:

  • Read and understand this Safeguarding Policy and The Scout Association's Safeguarding Policy & Procedures
  • Adhere to the Safeguarding Code of Conduct (Yellow Card) at all times
  • Complete mandatory safeguarding training within 30 days of appointment
  • Report any safeguarding concerns immediately
  • Maintain appropriate boundaries with young people
  • Act as positive role models

3.2 Group Leadership

The Group Lead Volunteers (Lawrence Roberts and Scott Shepherd) are responsible for:

  • Ensuring all volunteers understand and follow this policy
  • Ensuring appropriate recruitment and vetting procedures are followed
  • Providing support and guidance on safeguarding matters
  • Liaising with The Scout Association's Safeguarding Team when required
  • Ensuring regular review and updates of this policy

4. Code of Conduct

All adults in 28th Odiham (Dogmersfield and Crondall) Scout Group must follow The Scout Association's Safeguarding Code of Conduct (Yellow Card), which includes:

  • Treating all young people with dignity and respect
  • Always working in an open environment, avoiding private or unobserved situations
  • Never engaging in rough, physical or sexually provocative games
  • Respecting personal space and privacy
  • Not using inappropriate language or allowing others to do so unchallenged
  • Not consuming alcohol when directly responsible for young people
  • Not permitting young people under 18 to consume alcohol on Scouting activities
  • Never using illegal substances on Scouting activities

5. Recognising Abuse

All volunteers should be aware of the signs of abuse, which may include:

5.1 Physical Abuse

  • Unexplained injuries or burns
  • Fear of physical contact
  • Aggressive behaviour or severe withdrawal
  • Fear of going home or of parents being contacted

5.2 Emotional Abuse

  • Delayed physical, mental or emotional development
  • Sudden speech or sensory disorders
  • Inappropriate emotional responses
  • Fear of making mistakes or trying new activities

5.3 Sexual Abuse

  • Inappropriate sexual knowledge for age
  • Sexualised behaviour or language
  • Difficulty in making friends
  • Regression to younger behaviour

5.4 Neglect

  • Constant hunger or tiredness
  • Poor personal hygiene
  • Inadequate clothing
  • Frequent absence from activities

All safeguarding concerns must be reported immediately and always within 24 hours. If you are unsure whether something constitutes a safeguarding concern, report it anyway.

6.1 How to Report

  1. Primary Route: Contact The Scout Association's Safeguarding Team directly
  2. Alternative: Contact your Group Lead Volunteers who will escalate to the Safeguarding Team
  3. Emergency: If outside normal hours, call the Scouts Support Centre

  • Any action already taken
  • Your contact details

7. Confidentiality

Information about safeguarding concerns will only be shared with those who need to know in order to protect the young person. This may include:

  • The Scout Association's Safeguarding Team
  • Local Authority Children's Services
  • Police
  • Relevant commissioners and leaders

8. Training and Support

All volunteers must complete:

  • Safeguarding training within 30 days of appointment
  • Refresher training every 3 years
  • Additional training as required for specific roles

Support is available from The Scout Association's Safeguarding Team and local commissioners for any safeguarding concerns or questions.

9. Recruitment and Vetting

All adult volunteers undergo:

  • Application and reference checks
  • DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service) checks
  • Safeguarding and safety training
  • Probationary periods where appropriate

10. Online Safety

We recognise the importance of online safety and:

  • Follow The Scout Association's guidance on digital communication
  • Ensure appropriate supervision of online activities
  • Educate young people about online risks
  • Report any online safeguarding concerns

11. Anti-Bullying

We are committed to creating an environment where bullying is not tolerated. We will:

  • Promote respect and inclusion
  • Address bullying behaviour immediately
  • Support both victims and perpetrators appropriately
  • Work with parents/carers to resolve issues

12. Review and Updates

This policy will be reviewed annually or following any significant incident or change in legislation. All volunteers will be notified of any updates.
